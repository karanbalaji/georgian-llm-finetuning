# Presentation Deck Outline

40-minute walkthrough + 20-minute Q&A, cross-functional panel (AI Lab, Marketing, Events, Investment, Community). Roughly 2–3 min/slide, leaving room for the live prototype walkthrough to breathe.

1. **Title / who I am** — framed around a hybrid positioning: genuinely technical *and* a community/relationship builder, not one or the other. Sets up why this candidate can credibly own both parts of the case study.
2. **Current state of TL** — the problem statement: 1,000+ members, lightly active, not differentiated. Establish the stakes before pitching the fix.
3. **12-month vision statement** — Season 1, the default technical home for applied AI builders in Canada with an SF/NYC presence.
4. **Three-pillar framework** — Research & Thought Leadership / Talent Engagement & Access / Ecosystem Positioning, and the audiences each serves.
5. **The content engine** — Builder Series / Lab Series / Founder Talks, mapped to the three pillars; "Season 1 = Year 1" framing; Luma as the distribution layer already in place.
6. **Ambassador program** — Transferred Learnings Ambassadors, with the Stripe/Cerebras/NVIDIA/OpenAI precedent citations as evidence this is a proven mechanism, not a guess. This is the slide that answers "how do you scale into SF/NYC without headcount."
7. **Resourcing & cross-functional model** — who does what (Lead AI Advocate, AI Lab, Marketing/Events, RevOps/Investment, portfolio, partners), and the quarterly-flagship-plus-steady-content cadence.
8. **Year-1 success metrics** — active contributor rate, Lab Series reach, talent conversions, partnerships/deal flow, event NPS, ambassador health, Luma growth. Quality over vanity, stated explicitly.
9. **Transition into Part II** — "Here's Season 1's first Builder Series flagship, in practice." Bridges strategy into execution instead of treating Part II as a separate pitch.
10. **Render "Fine-Tune & Ship" Challenge** — why Render (the differentiation argument), the 8-week timeline, and the $10K budget breakdown. Talking points: the example tracks (support-ticket triage, domain summarizer, code-review assistant — all small, efficient models that serve on Render's standard infra, which is the toolkit's own pitch) and the sourcing funnel (TL/Luma, ambassadors, Vector + universities, Render's channels, targeted OSS outreach with a GitHub-link quality filter).
11. **Live walkthrough of the prototype** — the landing page and technical walkthrough page, including the embedded Render deploy button. This is the demo moment; leave real time for it rather than rushing.
12. **Why this hits both goals** — recap: toolkit gets real usage and case-study content, Render gets to see actual working code from candidates before ever seeing a resume. Close the loop with the program success metrics: toolkit adoption movement (stars/forks/clones), registration→submission conversion, deployed submissions, and — the headline number — fast-track interviews/hires signalled by Render.
13. **Close + Q&A prompt** — one clear ask (what you want the panel's reaction/decision to be), then open the floor. Q&A prep: the Risks & Mitigations table in `02-tactics.md` (low signups, drop-off, submission quality, judging load) is ready ammunition for the inevitable "what could go wrong" questions.
